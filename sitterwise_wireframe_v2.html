Cancellation confirmation

The honest message a caregiver sees when she tries to cancel a job she's accepted. The goal: discourage casual cancellations without being deceptive about what happens next.

Spec § 5.3 · Caregiver Portal

La Jolla
Heads up: This will count as a back-out on your reliability record. If something has come up that we can help with, text us at 619-663-4379 before canceling.

Required. This helps us understand whether there's something we should know about.

Never mind, go back Cancel this job
What happens after she cancels: The cancellation is logged on her assignment with timestamp and reason. Admin gets a notification and can leave it as Backed Out, mark it Excused with a note (genuine emergency), or reassign her to a different job (which converts the assignment to Reassigned, no back-out recorded).

Why we don't show back-out count directly: Showing "3 back-outs this year" feels punitive even when accurate. A reliability percentage with peer comparison conveys the same information in a way most caregivers will receive as feedback, not shame. The internal admin view keeps the raw counts for performance management.

Need a break? Pause your account.

Self-service hold/resume. When life happens, caregivers can pause themselves instead of ignoring job offers and accumulating back-outs.

Spec § 7.3 · Caregiver Portal · Phase 1

Take the time you need.

Pausing your account stops new job offers from coming through. Your profile, ratings, history, and Trustline progress are all preserved. Resume whenever you're ready — usually with one tap.

Cancel Pause my account
What happens when you resume: Just tap "Resume" on this same screen. Your status changes back to Active and job offers start coming through again. No admin intervention required for the routine case.
